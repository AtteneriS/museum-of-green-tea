The harvest

Imagine a bright, sunny field in a beautiful green valley. That's where it all begins. Green tea comes from a special plant called Camellia Sinensis. The best leaves are picked by hand, usually in the spring, when they're fresh and full of flavor after the long winter. This first pick is called "shincha", which means "new tea". The leaves are handled with care to make sure we get that fresh, delicious taste we all love.

The brew

Making green tea is a little different from other kinds of tea. You don't want to use boiling water, that can make it taste bitter. The secret is to use water that's hot but not bubbling, around 80°C (175°F). Pour that water over your tea leaves or bag and let it sit for just a minute or two. This gentle brewing brings out the sweet, grassy flavors without any bitterness. It's a simple step that makes a big difference.

The ritual

A cup of green tea is more than just a drink; it's a small moment of peace in your day. Take a break, find a quiet spot, and just focus on the warmth of the cup and the scent of the tea. It's a simple ritual that can help you slow down and relax. Whether you're curled up with a book or just enjoying the quiet, that small cup of green tea can be your little escape.
